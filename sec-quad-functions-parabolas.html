    REMARK: The vertex form of a quadratic function, \(f(x) = a\left(x-\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right)\right)^2 + c-\frac{b^2}{4a}\text{,}\) provides us with one method of finding the vertex. However, notice if we plug \(h = -\frac{b}{2a}\) into this form, the squared term becomes zero, which just leaves \(f\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right) = k\text{.}\) This gives us an alternate computational way to find the vertex directly from the standard form, \(f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c\text{.}\)
