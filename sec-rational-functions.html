    Section 2.5 Rational Functions (ALEKS 2.5)

    Definition 2.5.1. Rational function.

    Let \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) be polynomials where \(q(x) \neq 0\text{.}\) A function \(R(x)\) defined by
    \begin{equation*} R(x) =\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \end{equation*}
    is called a rational function. The domain of \(R(x)\) is all real numbers, excluding the real zeros of \(q(x)\) since division by zero is undefined.

    REMARK: The graph of a rational function will never cross a vertical asymptote, but may cross a horizontal asymptote. If there’s more than one vertical asymptote the graph of a rational function may cross a horizontal asymptote in the "middle".

    Definition 2.5.5. Vertical Asymptote.

    The line \(x = c\) is a vertical asymptote of the graph of a function \(f\) if \(f(x)\) approaches infinity or negative infinity as \(x\) approaches \(c\) from either side.
    🔗
    🔗

    Identify vertical asymptotes.

    Let \(f(x) =\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\text{,}\) where \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) have no common factors other than 1. To locate the vertical asymptotes of \(f(x)\text{,}\) determine the real numbers \(x\) where the denominator is zero, but the numerator is not zero.
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow c^{+}\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow \infty\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow c^{-}\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow \infty\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow c^{+}\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow -\infty\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow c^{-}\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow -\infty\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 2.5.7. Horizontal Asymptote.

    The line \(y = d\) is a horizontal asymptote of the graph of a function \(f\) if \(f(x)\) approaches \(d\) as \(x\) approaches infinity or negative infinity.
    🔗
    🔗

    Identify horizontal asymptotes.

    Let \(f\) be a rational function defined by
    \begin{equation*} f(x) =\displaystyle \frac{a_{n}x^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+...+a_{0}}{b_{m}x^{m}+b_{m-1}x^{m-1}+...+b_{0}} \end{equation*}
    where \(n\) is the degree of the numerator and \(m\) is the degree of the denominator.
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow \infty\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow d\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow \infty\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow d\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow -\infty\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow d\)
    🔗
    As \(x\rightarrow -\infty\text{,}\) \(f(x) \rightarrow d\)
    🔗

    Definition 2.5.10. Slant Asymptote.

    A rational function will have a slant asymptote (also known as an oblique asymptote) if the degree of the numerator is exactly one greater than the degree of the denominator.
    🔗
    🔗

    Identify Slant Asymptotes.

    To find an equation of a slant asymptote, divide the numerator of the function by the denominator. The quotient will be linear and the slant asymptote will be \(y=\) quotient.
    🔗
    🔗

    Graphing rational functions with "holes".

    If a rational function contains a common variable factor \(x-c\) in the numerator and the denominator, then there is a "hole" or removable discontinuity in the graph at that value of \(x\text{.}\) If the factor that makes the zero in the denominator cannot be cancelled out, then there is a vertical asymptote in the graph at that value of \(x\text{.}\)
